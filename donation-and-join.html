FAQ

Why is tree plantation important for the environment?
Tree plantation is crucial because trees produce oxygen, absorb harmful carbon dioxide (reducing greenhouse gases), filter pollutants from air and water, prevent soil erosion, and provide essential habitats and food for wildlife, making the planet healthier.
What are the benefits of planting trees for humans?
Humans benefit from trees through clean air, fresh food (fruits), medicines, timber, shade, reduced noise pollution, and economic opportunities (like selling shea butter or wood), while also helping to manage floods and build beautiful landscapes.
What is the best time to plant trees?
The best time for tree planting is generally during the monsoon or rainy season (around June-July in many regions), as this provides ample water for seedlings to establish strong root systems, though it can vary by location.
How do trees help fight climate change and pollution?
Trees are natural air purifiers, absorbing CO2 and releasing oxygen; their leaves trap dust and smog, while their roots stabilize soil, reducing runoff and helping manage urban flooding, thus combating climate change effects and pollution
What are some effective actions for tree plantation?
Effective actions include planting native, site-appropriate tree species, creating planting plans, recruiting volunteers, caring for seedlings, and planting trees in urban areas, schools, and roadsides to combat deforestation and beautify environments.
